EDUCATIONAL PHILOSOPHY

VM

"The Mind is Not a Vessel to be Filled, but a Fire to be Kindled."
— Plutarch

My educational philosophy is an intersection of two distinct but complementary frameworks: Constructionism and Humanistic Education. As a Mechanical Engineer turned educator, I approach the learning process as a design challenge where the student is the architect of their own understanding.

1

Learning by Making (Constructionism)

I believe that deep learning occurs when students are actively engaged in creating public entities. In my Math and Science classrooms, equations are not abstract rules to be memorized; they are tools for building. Whether it is coding a Python script to model a trajectory or constructing a truss bridge to understand vector forces, my students learn by doing. This aligns with the "Innovation for Life" ethos, as I encourage students to use their knowledge to solve tangible problems. I utilize Inquiry-Based Learning (IBL) to drive this process, starting units with provocative questions that necessitate mathematical tools to answer.

2

The Classroom as an Emotional Ecosystem (The "Happy School")

I adhere to the belief that Maslow must come before Bloom. A student who feels anxious or unsafe cannot engage in the higher-order thinking required for STEM innovation. My classroom is designed as a "Happy Environment" where psychological safety is paramount. I explicitly teach that failure is data—a concept native to engineering. By reframing mistakes as "iterations," I build resilience and banish the fear of being wrong. This emotional scaffolding allows students to take the intellectual risks necessary for creativity and growth.

3

Technology as an Amplifier

In the 21st century, technology is the vernacular of the learner. As an Educational Technology specialist, I view digital tools not as substitutes for teaching, but as amplifiers of human potential. I integrate AI, interactive simulations (Desmos, PhET), and collaborative platforms to differentiate instruction. This ensures that every learner, regardless of their starting point, has a personalized pathway to mastery. I aim to produce students who are not just consumers of technology, but ethical and proficient creators.

4

Global Citizenship

Finally, I believe that Math and Science are universal languages that bridge cultural divides. In a bilingual setting, I use these subjects to foster global citizenship, helping students understand that the laws of physics and the logic of mathematics are shared human heritages. I aim to nurture proactive, responsible citizens who use their skills to contribute positively to their community and the world.
